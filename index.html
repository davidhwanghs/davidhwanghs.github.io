Hyoseok (David) Hwang

Assistant Professor of Finance

Opus College of Business  ·  University of St. Thomas

Hyoseok (David) Hwang is an Assistant Professor of Finance at the University of St. Thomas Opus College of Business. He received his Ph.D. in Finance from Louisiana State University, his M.S. in Economics from the University of Nebraska, and his B.S. in Economics from the University of Seoul. His research sits at the intersection of financial intermediaries, corporate governance, and capital markets, with a unifying interest in how investors and creditors process and act on information — particularly when that information is adverse, local, or difficult to observe. One line of work examines local mutual funds and the informational advantages of geographic proximity, exploring how local institutional investors influence corporate innovation, social responsibility, and shareholder value. A second strand investigates securities class action lawsuits as a window into informed trading, asking how analysts, institutions, and underwriter networks anticipate and respond to corporate misconduct. A third line of research turns to credit markets, studying how CDS spreads and common institutional ownership reflect information about corporate wrongdoing and credit risk. More recent work extends these themes to emerging market phenomena, including SPACs, corporate crypto-asset disclosures, board diversity, and executive cultural distance in customer-supplier relationships.
